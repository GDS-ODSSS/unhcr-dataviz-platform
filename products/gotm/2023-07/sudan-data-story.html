Sudan data story

19 July 2023

At the beginning of 2023, Sudan hosted over 1.1 million refugees, who sought protection from conflict, insecurity, and climate catastrophes in their home countries. At the same time, Sudan also hosted nearly 3.8 million internally displaced people (IDPs) uprooted from their homes inside the country primarily due to armed clashes and communal conflict. All of this was before the events of mid-April 2023.

From 15 April 2023, armed clashes occurred between the Sudanese Army Forces and the Rapid Support Forces. This conflict erupted across Sudan, including Khartoum. Unsurprisingly, these clashes resulted in widespread displacement. In July, the United Nations reports that over 3 million people have been displaced since this conflict began in April. It is not currently possible to know how many of these 3 million were already among those displaced.

The majority of these 3 million people were displaced in the country. IOM reports, as of 9 July 2023, over 2.4 million people were internally displaced throughout Sudan. They mainly come Khartoum and Darfur. All the states in Sudan host IDPs, though the largest concentrations are in River Nile and Northern states, in the north of the country.

At the same time UNHCR reports that nearly 190,000 refugees are displaced within Sudan. These are individuals who sought safety in Sudan, but then were displaced again within the country due to this new fighting.

In addition to those displaced inside Sudan, hundreds of thousands fled Sudan to neighboring countries. Approximately 800,000 people left Sudan.

Of these, about 570,000 are refugees and asylum seekers in urgent need of international protection. 97% are Sudanese. The remaining are refugees of other nationalities who were in Sudan and now left. These refugees join already large refugee populations in Chad, Ethiopia, and elsewhere.

Over 160,000 of those fleeing Sudan are returning refugees. By far, the biggest group are South Sudanese who lived as refugees in Sudan, and now were forced, under duress, to return to South Sudan.

Additionally, approximately 80,000 are migrants and other third country nationals. These are people who were living in Sudan and were compelled to flee when fighting erupted. This is a diverse category of people including those evacuated by their respective governments at the beginning of the crisis, as well as other people on the move who were caught up in the violence.

The conflict in Sudan is having devastating consequences for nearly all people in Sudan and the surrounding countries. Among the most vulnerable are those forced to flee their homes. The different groups of people forcibly displaced have different short and long-term needs, but all are entitled to live in safety and dignity, and to return home.
